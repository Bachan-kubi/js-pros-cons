Slow lorises are a group of several species of trepsirrhine primates which make up the genus Nycticebus. They have a round head, narrow snout, large eyes, and a variety of distinctive coloration patterns that are species-dependent. The hands and feet of slow lorises have several adaptations that give them a pincer-like grip and enable them to grasp branches for long periods of time.
